"""
California Employment Law Compliance Checklist (2026)
Comprehensive list of required handbook policies and provisions.
"""

def get_checklist():
    """
    Returns the comprehensive CA employment law compliance checklist.
    Updated for 2026 with all current requirements.
    """
    
    checklist = """
# CALIFORNIA EMPLOYMENT LAW COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST (2026)

## CORE EMPLOYMENT POLICIES

1. **At-Will Employment Disclaimer** (Labor Code §2922)
   - Clear statement that employment is at-will
   - Can be terminated by either party at any time, with or without cause/notice
   - Only authorized person can modify (typically CEO/President in writing)

2. **Equal Employment Opportunity Policy** (Gov. Code §12940, FEHA)
   - Prohibits discrimination based on all protected classes
   - Must include: race, color, national origin, ancestry, religion, sex, gender, gender identity, gender expression, sexual orientation, age (40+), disability, medical condition, genetic information, marital status, military/veteran status, reproductive health decision-making
   - Statement of commitment to EEO

3. **Anti-Harassment Policy** (Gov. Code §12940, §12950)
   - Definitions of harassment (sexual, gender-based, and all protected classes)
   - Examples of prohibited conduct
   - Statement that harassment is illegal and will not be tolerated
   - Covers harassment by employees, managers, customers, vendors

4. **Harassment Complaint Procedure** (Gov. Code §12950)
   - Multiple reporting channels (supervisor, HR, executives, hotline)
   - Confidentiality provisions
   - Investigation process
   - Non-retaliation statement
   - Contact info for DFEH/CRD and EEOC

5. **Harassment Prevention Training Requirement** (Gov. Code §12950.1)
   - Employers with 5+ employees must provide training
   - 1 hour for all employees, 2 hours for supervisors
   - Every 2 years
   - Within 6 months of hire/promotion for supervisors
   - Within 30 days/100 hours for temporary/seasonal workers

## WAGE & HOUR REQUIREMENTS

6. **Minimum Wage Notice** (Labor Code §§1182.12, 1197.5)
   - Current CA minimum wage: $16.90/hour (effective Jan 1, 2026)
   - Exempt employee minimum salary: $70,304/year (2x minimum wage x 2080 hours)
   - Note any applicable city/county higher minimum wages
   - Industry-specific wages (fast food, healthcare if applicable)

7. **Wage Theft Protection Act Notice** (Labor Code §2810.5) - NEW
   - Required written notice to new hires at time of hire
   - Must include: pay rate, regular payday, employer name/address, paid sick leave info
   - Must notify employees within 7 days of any changes
   - Use Labor Commissioner form or equivalent

8. **Wage Statement Requirements** (Labor Code §226)
   - Itemized wage statement (pay stub) with each paycheck
   - Must include 11 items: dates of pay period, hours worked, rates of pay, gross/net wages, deductions, employer name/address, etc.
   - Must show available paid sick leave balance (or "unlimited")
   - Employees can request copies; employer must provide within 21 days

9. **Final Pay Timing Requirements** (Labor Code §§201-202) - NEW
   - Involuntary termination: All wages due immediately at termination
   - Voluntary resignation with 72+ hours notice: Pay on last day
   - Voluntary resignation without notice: Pay within 72 hours
   - Waiting time penalties up to 30 days wages for late payment

10. **Payroll Deductions Policy** (Labor Code §§221, 224, 400-410)
    - Prohibited deductions: uniforms, equipment, cash shortages (unless theft proven), business losses from simple negligence
    - Allowed deductions: taxes, court-ordered, employee-authorized benefits
    - Penalties: $200 + 25% of amount withheld per violation

11. **Meal Break Policy** (Labor Code §512, §226.7)
    - 30-minute unpaid meal break before end of 5th hour
    - Waiver allowed if workday ≤6 hours (must be mutual written agreement)
    - Second 30-minute meal break before end of 10th hour (can waive if ≤12 hours and first meal taken)
    - Must be duty-free (employee relieved of all duties)
    - Premium pay: 1 hour at regular rate for each violation

12. **Rest Break Policy** (Labor Code §226.7)
    - 10-minute paid rest break per 4 hours worked (or major fraction thereof)
    - Should be in middle of work period if practicable
    - Cannot be combined with meal break
    - Premium pay: 1 hour at regular rate for each violation

13. **Overtime Policy** (Labor Code §510)
    - Time-and-a-half: Over 8 hours/day OR over 40 hours/week
    - Double-time: Over 12 hours/day OR 8+ hours on 7th consecutive workday
    - 7th day overtime: Time-and-a-half for first 8 hours, double-time thereafter
    - All overtime must be authorized/approved

14. **Alternative Workweek Schedule** (Labor Code §511) - NEW (if applicable)
    - Allows schedules over 8 hours/day without daily overtime
    - Requires secret ballot election with 2/3 approval
    - Strict compliance procedures
    - Must be disclosed in handbook if used

15. **Independent Contractor Classification** (AB 5 - "ABC Test")
    - Policy on proper worker classification
    - ABC test factors for determining independent contractor status
    - Prohibition on misclassification

## LEAVE POLICIES

16. **Paid Sick Leave** (Labor Code §246)
    - Accrual: 1 hour per 30 hours worked (or front-load 24-40 hours/year)
    - Minimum carryover: Unused hours carry over (can cap at 48 hours/6 days)
    - Usage: Minimum 24 hours/year available
    - Covered reasons: Employee/family member illness, preventive care, safe time (domestic violence, sexual assault, stalking)
    - Notice and documentation requirements
    - Display of available balance on pay stub

17. **California Family Rights Act (CFRA)** (Gov. Code §12945.2)
    - Employers with 5+ employees (changed from 50 in 2021)
    - 12 weeks of protected leave in 12-month period
    - Eligibility: 12 months employment, 1,250 hours in previous 12 months
    - Covered reasons: Bonding with new child, serious health condition (self/family), military exigency, care for military servicemember
    - Expanded family member definitions (includes designated persons, adult children, grandparents, grandchildren, siblings)
    - Job restoration rights

18. **Pregnancy Disability Leave (PDL)** (Gov. Code §12945)
    - Employers with 5+ employees
    - Up to 4 months leave for pregnancy-related disability
    - No minimum service requirement
    - Reasonable accommodation for pregnancy-related conditions
    - Transfer rights for pregnant employees
    - Can run concurrently with or in addition to CFRA/FMLA

19. **Bereavement Leave** (Labor Code §12945.7) - NEW
    - Employers with 5+ employees
    - Up to 5 days of unpaid, job-protected leave
    - Death of covered family member or reproductive loss event
    - Eligibility: 30 days of employment
    - Covered family members: spouse, child, parent, sibling, grandparent, grandchild, domestic partner

20. **Crime Victim Leave** (Labor Code §230, §230.1) - AB 406 Enhanced 2026
    - Time off for employees who are crime victims or whose family member is a crime victim
    - Covers: Court appearances, victim services, counseling, etc.
    - Protected from retaliation
    - Notice requirements (Crime Victims Notice from CRD - must post and distribute)

21. **Jury Duty & Witness Leave** (Labor Code §230)
    - Protected time off for jury duty and appearing as witness
    - No required pay (but cannot penalize employee)
    - Cannot retaliate

22. **Military Leave** (Military and Veterans Code §§394-395.10)
    - Protected leave for military service and training
    - Reinstatement rights
    - Covers spouse leave to be with deployed spouse on leave

23. **Voting Leave** (Elections Code §14000)
    - Up to 2 hours paid time off to vote if insufficient time outside work hours
    - Must request in advance

24. **School Activities Leave** (Labor Code §230.8)
    - Up to 40 hours/year for parents to participate in school/child care activities
    - Applies to employers with 25+ employees
    - Unpaid (can use vacation/PTO)

25. **Organ/Bone Marrow Donor Leave** (Labor Code §1508-1513)
    - Up to 30 business days/year for organ donation
    - Up to 5 business days/year for bone marrow donation
    - Paid leave (with verification)

## WORKPLACE RIGHTS & PROTECTIONS

26. **Personnel Records Access** (Labor Code §1198.5)
    - Current/former employees can inspect personnel records
    - Employer must provide access within 30 days of request
    - Can inspect up to once/year while employed
    - Can have representative inspect records
    - Penalties: $750 per violation

27. **Expense Reimbursement** (Labor Code §2802)
    - Reimburse necessary business expenses
    - Covers: Mileage, cell phone, tools, equipment, supplies
    - Must be paid on regular payday

28. **PAGA Notice** (Labor Code §2699.3)
    - Private Attorneys General Act
    - Inform employees of right to file PAGA claims for Labor Code violations
    - Notice of employee's right to represent State in labor law enforcement

29. **Lactation Accommodation** (Labor Code §1031)
    - Reasonable break time to express breast milk
    - Private space (not bathroom) close to work area
    - Access to refrigerator or personal cooler
    - Applies to all employers
    - 2024 amendments: Enhanced privacy/safety requirements, lactation room standards

30. **Whistleblower Protection** (Labor Code §1102.5)
    - Employees can report legal violations without retaliation
    - Covers reporting to government agencies, internal complaints
    - Strong anti-retaliation protections
    - Encouragement to report violations

31. **Anti-Retaliation Policy** (Labor Code §98.6, §1102.5)
    - Prohibition on retaliation for exercising legal rights
    - Covers: Wage complaints, harassment complaints, leave requests, whistleblowing, union activity
    - Reporting mechanism for retaliation complaints

32. **Workplace Violence Prevention Plan** (Labor Code §6401.9) - NEW 2024
    - Written plan required for all employers (effective July 1, 2024)
    - Hazard identification and correction procedures
    - Employee training (annual)
    - Violent incident log
    - Employee participation in development/implementation

## 2025-2026 NEW REQUIREMENTS

33. **AI/Automated Decision Systems Policy** (SB 1001 - NEW 2025)
    - Disclosure of use of AI in employment decisions (hiring, promotions, performance)
    - Employee rights regarding automated systems
    - Non-discrimination requirements
    - Record retention: 4 years for ADS-related records

34. **Emergency Contact Designation** (SB 294 - NEW 2025)
    - Allow employees to designate emergency contacts
    - Employer must notify emergency contact if employee arrested/detained at work
    - Confidentiality protections

35. **Workers' Rights Notice** (SB 294 - NEW 2025)
    - Comprehensive notice of employee rights under CA law
    - Must distribute annually and to new hires

36. **Cannabis-Use Protections** (AB 2188 - Effective 2024) - NEW
    - Cannot discriminate against employees for off-duty cannabis use
    - Restrictions on cannabis drug testing (cannot use tests detecting non-psychoactive metabolites)
    - Exceptions: Federal contractors, positions requiring federal background checks, building/construction trades

## HIRING & EMPLOYMENT PRACTICES

37. **Criminal History in Hiring** ("Ban the Box" - Gov. Code §12952)
    - Cannot ask about criminal history before conditional job offer
    - After conditional offer, must follow specific procedures before denying based on criminal history
    - Individualized assessment required
    - Notice and opportunity to respond

38. **Salary History Ban** (Labor Code §432.3)
    - Cannot ask applicants about salary history
    - Cannot rely on salary history in hiring/pay decisions
    - Must provide pay scale upon request

39. **Pay Scale Disclosure in Job Postings** (Labor Code §432.3) - Enhanced 2023
    - Employers with 15+ employees must include pay scale in all job postings
    - "Pay scale" = salary or hourly wage range employer reasonably expects to pay

40. **Social Media Privacy** (Labor Code §980)
    - Cannot require employees/applicants to disclose personal social media usernames/passwords
    - Cannot require access to personal social media accounts
    - Cannot retaliate for refusing to provide access

41. **Immigration Rights** (AB 450 - Immigrant Worker Protection Act)
    - Cannot provide voluntary consent for immigration enforcement to access non-public areas without judicial warrant
    - Must post notice within 72 hours of receiving Notice of Inspection from immigration agency
    - Must distribute "Know Your Rights" notice annually and to new hires
    - Already covered in Emergency Contact above: Allow employees to designate emergency contact for arrest/detention

42. **Non-Compete Agreement Ban** (Business & Professions Code §16600)
    - Non-compete agreements void in California
    - Handbook should clarify that non-competes will not be enforced
    - May use: Non-solicitation, confidentiality, intellectual property agreements (if reasonable)

## WORKPLACE SAFETY & COMPLIANCE

43. **Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP)** (Labor Code §6401.7)
    - Written workplace safety program required
    - Hazard identification and correction
    - Training and communication
    - Record keeping

44. **Workers' Compensation Notice** (Labor Code §3550-3553)
    - Notice of workers' compensation coverage
    - How to report injuries
    - Contact information for claims

45. **California WARN Act** (Labor Code §§1400-1408)
    - Mass layoff/plant closure notification
    - Broader than federal WARN Act
    - 60-day advance notice to employees, EDD, local workforce agencies
    - Applies to employers with 75+ employees

## POSTING & NOTICE REQUIREMENTS

46. **Required Workplace Postings**
    - Minimum wage poster
    - Payday notice
    - Safety and health protection poster
    - Emergency phone numbers
    - Whistleblower protections
    - Workers' compensation notice
    - Discrimination/harassment notices (DFEH)
    - Pregnancy disability leave notice
    - Family care and medical leave notice (CFRA/FMLA)
    - Paid sick leave notice
    - Human trafficking notice (certain businesses)
    - Crime Victims Notice (CRD)
    - Wage theft protection notice (for all new hires)

---

## SUMMARY
Total Requirements: 46 major policy areas

Key 2025-2026 Updates:
- Minimum wage: $16.90/hour (Jan 1, 2026)
- Exempt salary threshold: $70,304/year
- AI/Automated Decision Systems disclosure
- Emergency contact designation
- Workers' rights notice
- Bereavement leave (5+ employees)
- Cannabis-use protections
- Workplace Violence Prevention Plan
- Pay scale disclosure in job postings (15+ employees)
- Enhanced crime victims protections
"""
    
    return checklist
